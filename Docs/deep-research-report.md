## Sensor/control architecture and synthetic hunting that preserves motion law

### Why TE-style observability matters even if piston position is measured directly

Direct piston sensing means you can detect motion-law drift without disassembly, which is excellent. However, to **act** (index, micro-dither) without corrupting the motion law, you need a state estimate that separates:

- slow wear (irreversible),
- thermal expansion (temperature-driven, reversible),
- elastic compliance under load (torque/pressure-driven, reversible).

Transmission error measurement is a recognized validation quantity in gear dynamics; NASA developed direct TE measurement systems and notes TE can be mapped over rotation and transformed into spectra at mesh frequencies for vibration/noise analysis. ŅłĆciteŅłéturn16search3Ņłéturn16search14Ņłü  

### Observer structure to remove thermal/elastic components

Model the equivalent phase error as:
\[
\Delta\phi(\theta,t)=\Delta\phi_{\text{wear}}(\theta,t)+\Delta\phi_{\text{elas}}(\theta,t)+\Delta\phi_{\text{therm}}(\theta,t)+\eta(\theta,t)
\]

Recommended practical implementation (1ŌĆō2 engine program complexity is acceptable):

- Represent \(\Delta\phi_{\text{wear}}(\theta,t)\) as a **slow random walk** in a low-order periodic basis aligned to your sector repetition:
  - Fourier components up to K harmonics per sector, or
  - spline knots in ╬Ė with enforced periodicity per sector.
- Model \(\Delta\phi_{\text{elas}}(\theta,t)\) as a regressor on load proxies (torque estimate, cylinder pressure estimate, or measured shaft strain if available).
- Model \(\Delta\phi_{\text{therm}}(\theta,t)\) as a regressor on measured temperatures (oil supply/return, ring housing, bearings).
- Estimate with an EKF/UKF or recursive least squares with different process-noise models (wear Ōē½ thermal Ōē½ elastic).
